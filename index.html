FROM THE SKY TO THE STARS

WHAT IS IRDBA?

The International Rocketry and Drone Building Association is a youth-led initiative that brings together students, engineers, and researchers to develop drones and rockets. Founded in Kazakhstan, IRDBA aims to combine the country’s aerospace heritage with modern innovation, turning our nation into a hub for practical aerospace education and development.

GOALS & MISSION

Our mission is to create opportunities for young people in Kazakhstan to engage with aerospace engineering. We aim to train future experts by providing hands-on experience in building rockets and drones, organizing competitions, and collaborating with global aerospace communities.

Ultimately, IRDBA strives to develop Kazakhstan’s domestic drone-making sector, transforming the country from an importer of technology into a producer and innovator. In the long term, we plan to build an international network of young engineers and researchers, expanding IRDBA beyond Kazakhstan and positioning it as a global hub for aerospace collaboration.

RELEVANCE & INNOVATION

IRDBA

Kazakhstan is home to Baikonur Cosmodrome, the world’s first and largest spaceport. However, many young people lack access to aerospace education at a practical level. IRDBA fills this gap by introducing innovative, project-based learning that allows students to design, test, and apply aerospace technologies within their own environment.

PRACTICAL SIGNIFICANCE

DRONES ARE THE FUTURE

IRDBA

Drone and rocketry technologies are vital for Kazakhstan’s future. Drones can be used in agriculture (crop monitoring), ecology (forest fire detection, environmental studies), and infrastructure (surveying remote areas). Rockets and aerospace education inspire new generations of engineers, directly supporting Kazakhstan’s technological and economic growth.

RESOURCES NEEDED

To turn IRDBA into a working hub of aerospace innovation, we need both space and tools. A coworking and prototyping lab will serve as the foundation, equipped with 3D printers (Creality Ender 3 V3, Prusa MK3S+) for custom drone and rocket parts, CNC tools, and soldering stations.

For electronics and control systems, we require Arduino boards, Raspberry Pi units, Pixhawk flight controllers, and sensors such as GPS modules, barometers, and IMUs. Camera modules (Raspberry Pi Camera v3, FLIR Lepton thermal sensors) will support drone payload development.

Model rocketry will use commercially certified motors (Estes, Aerotech), flight computers (Altus Metrum EasyMini), and GPS trackers (u-blox NEO-6M). Drones will be powered by brushless motors (T-Motor, EMAX), LiPo batteries (Tattu), and controlled with telemetry radios (FrSky, Radiomaster).

BUSINESS MODEL

THE GRAND VISION

IRDBA will operate as an educational and research network. Our activities include training programs, youth competitions, and collaborations with companies that need drone-based services in agriculture, logistics, and safety. Over time, we plan to develop a membership system and international partnerships to sustain operations.

EXPECTED RESULTS

Within the first year, IRDBA expects to:

  1. Build and test functioning drones and small rockets in Kazakhstan.
  2. Train dozens of students in practical aerospace engineering.
  3. Organize public events to showcase projects and attract new talent.
  4. Create international recognition for Kazakhstan’s youth-driven aerospace initiatives.

MONTH ROADMAP

MONTH 1
